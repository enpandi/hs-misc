the Laplacian

differential operator

sum of spatial second partial derivatives

vector -> scalar

analogous to a second derivative

approximate interpretation: at a point = the average difference between the point's neighbors and the point

Laplace's equation

approximate interpretation: "every point in the domain of has a value equal to the average value of its neighbors"

elliptic partial differential equation

generalizes to Poisson's equation

"potential theory" is the study of functions that satisfy Laplace's equation, ("harmonic functions")

useful in many branches of physics (e.g. force fields, hydrodynamics, steady-state temperatures)

wave equation

describes waves (which are everywhere in physics and life, very useful)

heat equation

approximate interpretation: at any given point in space-time, the value at that point approaches the average value of its neighbors

describes heat conduction (obviously) as well as some other stuff like Brownian motion

transport equation
